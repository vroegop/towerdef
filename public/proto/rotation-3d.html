Why this is the "free rotation" answer

This is a stand-in low-poly figure (body + head + a snout cone so you can read its facing), not the real orc — building the actual orc in 3D is the work. But it shows the payoff: any heading with no baked frames, plus dynamic lighting that reacts as it turns.

Toon shade steps the lighting into bands so it reads like hand-drawn cel art instead of smooth plastic. Pixelate renders at low resolution and upscales, faking the pixel-art look over 3D. Combine them and a 3D model can sit convincingly next to 2D pixel sprites.

Trade-off vs sprites: needs a modeller + per-frame GPU cost, but kills the angle-count problem entirely and unlocks the 36-direction pre-render as a bonus (just screenshot this from N angles).
