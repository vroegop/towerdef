What you're looking at

This pawn is built from 26 horizontal slices (cross-sections, bottom→top), each drawn rotated by the same heading and nudged up the screen. The eyes/snout live at a fixed spot in the model, so spinning the stack makes the face orbit — it reads as a character turning to any angle, no baked frames.

Layer height = how far apart slices sit on screen, i.e. the fake camera tilt (0 = pure top-down, higher = more "3/4"). Slice count trades smoothness for fill-cost.

Trade-off: genuinely free rotation, cheap, and a charming chunky look — but it's a specific aesthetic, and art must be authored as slices (your hand-drawn 3/4 orcs aren't). Great for props, pickups, and blocky enemies.
