🧭 Directions & Free Rotation

How many baked angles is "enough", and why a flat sprite can't truly free-rotate. ← game · orc prototype

A · Baked directions — "ideal" sprite (has every angle)
8

Drag to change how many baked angles exist. The arrow = a character rendered at that facing. At 4 it's choppy; by ~16–32 the snapping is invisible and it reads as smooth rotation. This is the 3D→sprite render trick (Diablo/GTA). Code is trivial; the cost is authoring this many angles.

B · The real orc art
4-dir snap (correct)
naive free-rotate

Same orc orbiting the hero. Left snaps to the nearest of the 4 hand-drawn facings — always correct, but limited. Right rotates the single front-frame freely — smooth, but visibly wrong: the 3/4 perspective tilts, the shadow spins, the orc never actually turns to face sideways. That's why flat 3/4 art can't free-rotate.
